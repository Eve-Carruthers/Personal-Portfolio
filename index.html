About Me

Where science meets creativity, and passion drives impact

I've always been fascinated by the question: How can we use science to make a real difference? Whether I'm investigating how climate change affects endangered Arctic plants with the Royal Society, leading my STEM Racing team to championship victories, or performing as concertmaster for two orchestras, I'm driven by the belief that meaningful impact happens at the intersection of disciplines.

My work spans from studying physiological responses in Arctic alpine plants to developing AI-driven approaches in oncology research. I earned an A* (100%) on my HPQ investigating AI in cancer research, and I'm proud to have won a silver medal at the International Computational Physics Olympiad representing Jersey on the global stage.

Currently, I'm involved with the American Lung Cancer Screening Initiative, exploring how AI can improve early detection and patient outcomes. I also placed as runner-up at the Neuro-Cardiological Innovation Summit, where we proposed cross-disciplinary diagnostic solutions.

As Team Principal and Head of R&D for STEM Racing, I've learned that great leadership isn't just about winning—it's about building a culture where everyone can innovate. I mentor the next generation of engineers, guide strategy, and push the boundaries of what's possible with CAD and CFD simulations.

Beyond racing, I serve on the Jersey Youth Parliament, present at government conferences on STEM education and youth policy, and hold leadership roles as Science Prefect and Senior Editorial Lead at The Weekly Spaceman, where I translate complex science into compelling stories for global audiences.

Music has always been my creative counterbalance to scientific rigor. As concertmaster for two orchestras and a two-time runner-up at Jersey Young Musician of the Year, I bring the same precision and passion to performance that I apply in the lab. I'm also a member of the Jersey Youth Symphony Orchestra and perform across jazz, chamber, and choir ensembles.

Sport keeps me grounded. Whether I'm swimming laps, scaling climbing walls, or cycling through challenging routes, I channel determination and grit into every pursuit. Pushing myself physically reminds me that resilience is a muscle, and it's one I'm proud to flex in every part of my life.

I believe the most powerful innovations happen when we refuse to stay in our lanes. Science, art, leadership—these aren't separate paths. They're different lenses through which we can understand and improve the world. My goal isn't just to excel in each field, but to find the connections between them and use those insights to create something truly meaningful.

What Drives Me

The work I'm most passionate about

Royal Society Research

I'm leading physiological studies on endangered Arctic alpine plants, presenting my findings at Royal Society conferences and the Summer Science Exhibition. It's incredible to see how climate change affects these resilient species.

STEM Racing Team Principal

As Team Principal, I direct R&D for our champion team, innovating CAD/CFD strategies and mentoring the next generation of engineers. Watching my teammates grow is just as rewarding as winning.

Healthcare Innovation

I'm advancing neurocardiological solutions and contributing to the American Lung Cancer Screening Initiative with an AI-first approach. Using technology to save lives is what gets me out of bed in the morning.

Youth Advocacy

Winning the Jersey TechAwards, presenting at government conferences, and serving on the Jersey Youth Parliament lets me drive real change for young people in STEM. Our voices matter.
